# Target Segments

> Five concrete buyer segments ranked by fit. For each: company profile, pain intensity, budget, champion, blocker, sales motion, and what wins the deal.

## Ranking overview

| Rank | Segment | Fit (1-10) | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Privacy-conscious mid-tech (50-500 ppl) | **9** | Largest, fastest-growing pain, easiest to reach, local-first story resonates |
| 2 | Boutique professional services (law, arch, acct, consulting, 10-100 ppl) | **8** | High pain, document-heavy, will pay for productivity, NDAs are a perfect wedge |
| 3 | Biotech / pharma R&D (50-500 ppl) | **8** | Extreme pain, IP-sensitive (local-first is decisive), budget exists |
| 4 | Startups scaling past "single Slack workspace" (30-200 ppl) | **7** | Growing fast, decision-maker accessible, but cost-sensitive |
| 5 | Enterprise F500 (500+ ppl, security review process) | **4** | Big budget but slow sales cycle, procurement-heavy, Glean/Copilot already incumbent |

## Segment 1: Privacy-conscious mid-tech (PRIMARY)

**Company profile:**
- 50-500 employees
- Engineering, product, ops heavy
- Uses SharePoint or Google Workspace as primary docs
- Has compliance officer who's already blocked ChatGPT for Work, Copilot, or Notion AI
- Tech-forward culture; employees already use Notion, Linear, Slack
- Likely in: B2B SaaS, fintech, healthtech, developer tools

**Why this pain is acute:**
- 10+ SaaS apps, employees constantly searching across them
- Compliance officer keeps blocking "AI tools" — but everyone *wants* AI for search
- "Local-first semantic search" is the perfect compromise: AI power + no data leaving

**Budget:**
- $15/user/month is easy
- $25/user/month is the stretch — they'll negotiate but say yes
- Annual contract: $9k-$150k depending on size

**Decision-maker (champion):**
- Head of IT, or VP of Engineering, or "DevTools/Mobility" budget owner
- Sometimes the Chief of Staff (very common in 100-300 person companies)

**Blocker:**
- Security review (1-4 weeks). Our local-first story is the antidote.
- Procurement (PO process). For <$10k/yr, credit-card buy is common — sell self-serve.

**Sales motion:**
- **Self-serve first.** Individual users install the Mac app, evangelize internally.
- **PLG funnel:** in-app banner "Want your whole team to use Telme? Click here." → team signup → free trial of Business tier for 14 days → sales-assist call if >10 seats.
- **Content-led.** SEO-optimized blog: "How to do semantic search across SharePoint without sending data to the cloud" → ranks for high-intent searches.

**What wins the deal:**
- "You already block Copilot and ChatGPT for Work. Telme gives your team the AI productivity without sending any data to a third party. The Mac app does the indexing locally."
- Demo: 2-minute video showing semantic search across their actual SharePoint.

**Realistic conversion:** 2-5% of free individual users → paid Pro; 0.5-1% of free → Business team plan.

## Segment 2: Boutique professional services

**Company profile:**
- 10-100 employees (small partnerships + boutique firms)
- Document-heavy: contracts, briefs, drawings, decks
- Heavy NDA / client-confidentiality constraints
- Examples: architecture firm (50 ppl, 20 years of project files), patent law firm (15 attorneys, 100k+ patent docs), consulting firm (30 consultants, 200+ active clients)

**Why this pain is acute:**
- "Where's the 2019 Acme engagement deck?" is asked 10×/day
- One lost document = $10k+ in re-billing
- Their existing tools (Windows Search, SharePoint native search) are objectively terrible

**Budget:**
- $20-$30/user/month — they have healthy margins
- Easier to sell annual contracts ($3k-$30k/yr)
- Often bill it back to clients as overhead

**Decision-maker:**
- Managing partner, COO, head of knowledge management
- Sometimes the office IT person (for small firms)

**Blocker:**
- "We've tried this before" — most have purchased DevonThink, Worldox, iManage and been disappointed
- Compliance: many are bound by client NDAs that restrict where data goes

**Sales motion:**
- **Direct outreach.** LinkedIn ABM to managing partners at firms of the right size.
- **Vertical-specific landing pages.** "Telme for architecture firms" / "Telme for patent attorneys."
- **Webinars with vertical associations.** AIA (AIA Contract Documents), ABA Tech Show, etc.

**What wins the deal:**
- "Your partners spend 30 min/day finding documents. At $500/hr bill rate, that's $25k/partner/year wasted. Telme is $240/partner/year. ROI in week 1."
- Demo: search across a sample NDA-protected corpus (anonymized)
- Reference: similar-sized firm with similar practice area

**Realistic conversion:** 1-2% of outreach → closed. Higher if you have a referral from a peer firm.

## Segment 3: Biotech / pharma R&D

**Company profile:**
- 50-500 employees at a biotech / CRO / pharma R&D site
- Heavy use of SharePoint for lab notebooks, protocols, experiment data
- Strict IP / compliance: SOC 2, possibly HIPAA, possibly 21 CFR Part 11
- Examples: 100-person biotech with 5 years of experiments; 300-person CRO

**Why this pain is acute:**
- "Show me all experiments where we tried inhibiting target X with mechanism Y" — a *semantic* query
- Lab notebooks span years; people leave; institutional knowledge is fragile
- Existing solutions (Benchling, Dotmatics) are $50-200k/year and search poorly across documents

**Budget:**
- $30-50/user/month
- $50k-$500k/year contracts possible
- Often have a "scientific computing" or "data science" budget line

**Decision-maker:**
- VP of R&D, Head of Data Science, Chief Scientific Officer
- Sometimes IT director (if regulated)

**Blocker:**
- **Compliance validation.** 3-12 months. Our local-first story + SOC 2 roadmap is the answer.
- Validation by IT security team
- Procurement (large contracts go through legal review)

**Sales motion:**
- **Outbound + conferences.** BIO International, ASHG, PEGS. Booth + demos.
- **Content:** scientific publications about semantic search for life sciences
- **Partnerships:** with ELN vendors (Benchling, Dotmatics) as a "search layer"
- **Long sales cycle** (6-18 months) but high LTV ($50k+/yr)

**What wins the deal:**
- "Your competitors are using AI to accelerate drug discovery. Your IP rules prevent cloud AI. Telme gives you AI search that runs in your environment."
- Demo: search across a sample lab notebook set (de-identified)
- Validation: security whitepaper, SOC 2 audit (planned)

**Realistic conversion:** Very low (1 in 50 outreaches) but high LTV. Worth pursuing only after segments 1 & 2 are working.

## Segment 4: Startups scaling past "single Slack workspace"

**Company profile:**
- 30-200 employees, 1-3 years after Series A
- Document chaos: Slack, Notion, Drive, GitHub, Linear all in play
- Engineers + PMs ask "where do we keep X?" in Slack daily
- Budget-conscious but growing fast

**Why this pain is acute:**
- Adding employees = knowledge getting harder to find
- New hires take 3-6 months to ramp because docs are scattered
- Founder/CTO personally feels the pain

**Budget:**
- $8-12/user/month is the sweet spot
- Annual $3k-$30k
- Will pay for productivity if the ROI is clear

**Decision-maker:**
- CTO, VP Engineering, Head of People
- Sometimes the founder themselves (small enough to be hands-on)

**Blocker:**
- "We use Notion for everything, that's enough" (until they have to find a 2-year-old doc)
- Cost sensitivity — they have runway to manage
- Compete with free: Slack search, Notion search, Spotlight

**Sales motion:**
- **PLG.** Individual users install the Mac app. Onboarding shows them "you also have 47 docs in this Slack channel — add Slack as a source."
- **In-product upgrade prompts.** "Your team is sharing the same searches. Upgrade to Team plan."
- **Content:** "The startup knowledge management problem" — blog + Twitter

**What wins the deal:**
- "Your new hires are spending 30% of their first month asking 'where is X?' Telme pays for itself in ramp time."
- "Your team is 80 people. Each is losing 1 hour/day to search. That's $200k/yr. Telme is $10k/yr."

**Realistic conversion:** 3-7% of free team → paid. Very viral if it works.

## Segment 5: Enterprise F500 (DEFER)

**Company profile:**
- 500+ employees, F500
- Long procurement cycles (6-18 months)
- Strict vendor review (SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, etc.)

**Why we should NOT pursue this segment first:**
- Slowest sales cycle
- Glean, Copilot, Hebbia already have budget allocated
- Procurement requires certifications we don't have yet
- Even with local-first, the security review is heavy

**When to pursue:**
- After we have SOC 2 Type 1 (~$30k, 6-month process)
- After we have a proven mid-market motion
- After we have case studies from mid-tech

**Sales motion (when ready):**
- ABM + partner channel
- Industry events (Gartner IAM, Microsoft Ignite)
- Direct enterprise sales hire

**Realistic conversion:** 1 in 100 outreaches, but $50k-$500k ACV.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) summary card

**Best fit (early focus):**
- 50-500 person B2B tech company
- Uses SharePoint or Google Workspace
- Has blocked "AI tools" for compliance
- CTO or Head of IT is the decision maker
- $10-20/user/month is invisible budget

**Deal size:**
- Starter: 25 seats × $15 × 12 mo = **$4,500/yr**
- Mid-market: 100 seats × $15 × 12 mo = **$18,000/yr**
- Enterprise: 500 seats × $15 × 12 mo = **$90,000/yr**

**Sales cycle:**
- Self-serve (PLG): <30 days
- Mid-market: 30-90 days
- Enterprise: 6-18 months (later)

**Win rate by motion:**
- Self-serve signup → paid: 2-5%
- Self-serve team plan → upgrade to Business: 10-20% (if security review passes)
- Outbound to qualified ICP: 5-10%
- Cold outbound: 1-3%
